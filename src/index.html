The pursuit of tenant happiness

“Of course our main goal is capital savings. We want to increase property value, but energy efficiency is really about making people happy,” says Phillip Raffi, National Energy and Sustainability Manager. “It’s about the overall health and wellness of tenants. Air quality has a lot to do with that, lighting has a lot to do with that.”

Raffi cites a recent LED retrofit at 150 Bloor Street West, a 665,000-square-foot mixed-use property, as a prime example. “Lighting’s the easiest upgrade because it’s fast. Everyone can see the difference right away and Toronto Hydro provides a great incentive,” he says. “But it has many non-monetary benefits as well – the property looks so much nicer and people feel more alive in the space. We went from really blotchy light to beautiful, uniform daylighting.”

Colliers gave tenants a choice of LEDs, letting them live with different colour indexes, from warmer to cooler, brighter lighting (3000K to 5000K), for a few weeks before the full retrofit went ahead. “Instead of just doing it and hoping they’d be happy, we piloted different options,” Raffi explains. “We want to get it right the first time.”

“Saving energy makes financial sense and improves the building. And reducing your carbon footprint in the process – that’s a trifecta.”

Building strong business relationships

For Colliers, getting building owner/operator buy-in for upgrades is the first step. “We’re a third-party property manager, so we have to align our goals with the unique goals of each building,” Raffi says. How does he balance the twenty-plus buildings he manages in the Toronto core? Raffi says consistency is key. “There’s no secret and there’s no way around it; you have to put in the time. I try to visit a different property every other week or so and do a building walkthrough with the operator. It makes a big difference.” He also stays in touch with an informal email newsletter, sharing news about technologies and incentives.

(L-R) Becki Kimpton, Project Manger; Alif Dharssi, Team Lead, Business Solutions; Phillip Raffi, National Energy and Sustainability Manager, Colliers International

“Property owners and operators are extremely busy. My role is to be a knowledge hub and help take action items off their plates. If I can alleviate their workload, that’s the value I bring.”

Achieving BOMA® best certification

Parking garage lighting was another easy improvement at 150 Bloor, followed by adding CO sensors to ventilation fans and VFDs to supply and return fans and pumps. The entire suite of upgrades eventually earned the building a BOMA BEST® Platinum certification, a mark of distinction earned by only 3% of buildings that apply.

Raffi says organizing upgrades around a green building certification is an effective catalyst for change. “It allows you to engage a broad spectrum of stakeholders and it’s a great way to get people excited about saving energy,” he says, noting the advantage of integrating a set of upgrades into capital budgets.

Not only does green building certification typically reduce energy, water and maintenance costs, but it can also attract AAA tenants as they perceive it as a sign of a well-managed building.
